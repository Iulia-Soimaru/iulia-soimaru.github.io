What is Pair Programming

What is "Pair programming"?
It is a way of working on web or software development project, where 2 people are working together on the same problem or project. Two people usually take their roles, where one is “driver” who actually is writing a code and the second one is “navigator” who is reviewing if each line of code is written correctly and makes sense.
    It is very important to DBC for:
  1. Sharing information and knowledge
  2. Learning how other people solve problems
  3. Helping better understanding the project
  4. Learning how to communicate effectively
  5. Bringing us more ideas
  6. Catching mistakes at the right moment
Driver vs. Navigator
Driver and navigator are thinking about the project, and give new ideas during the work process. But there is a small difference in their roles:

“Driver is a person who is in charge of writing a code.”
“Navigator is a person who is guiding the work process, and reviewing the code that driver is writing.”
